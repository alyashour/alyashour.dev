About Me

My name is Aly Ashour, and I'm a software engineering student at the University of Western Ontario.

I live to work with computers and technology and odds are if it's metal or silicon I want my hands on it. I write lots of code and chew on cables and keyboards both on my own and on any team that'll let me in (and even some that don't).

Passions

I'm passionate about building systems that solve real-world problems — the kind that exist outside of the computer or the classroom. I'm especially drawn to:

  • Autonomy — not just "self-driving" as a concept, but how we can teach machines to think and the problems they can solve.
  • Applied Engineering — systems that help people, improve lives, or change the world for the better. I want to build tools that matter.
  • Global solutions — especially for public systems like transit, safety, or accessibility. We have a responsibility to create for the whole world.
  • Difficult problems — I'm fascinated by ambiguity and drawn to difficulty. An "unsolved" problem is just a signal saying there is more to see here. I hope to push the technical boundary further than we thought possible.

Besides that I adore space, physics, and ancient history, I play chess, some video games, football (not the american one), and my favourite song is Fragile by Laufey.
